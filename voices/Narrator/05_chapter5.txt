Sunny fell to his knees, breathless, body broken yet exhilarated. No Memory appeared, only pain and cold.
The larva’s corpse still chained them. Shifty and Scholar untangled links while chaos raged further away.
Several maimed bodies began to move. Four more larvae rose, fangs glistening.
A larva lunged — but a sword flashed, beheading it in one strike.
The young soldier fought on, then threw something to Sunny.
It was a bent iron rod. A key to the shackles.
With a click, Sunny was free. He rubbed his wrists, eyes gleaming with dark intent.
The third larva died under the soldier’s blade. Shifty and Scholar argued over fleeing.
Sunny knew Scholar was right. The mountain itself was deadlier than hunger or chains.
